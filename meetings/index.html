Meetings

The ISNET series of international meetings started in 2012 with a one-day workshop in Krakow, Poland and has been held since in both Europe and the United States. See this editorial in the Journal of Physics G and this article in Nuclear Physics News on the first ISNET workshop in Krakow.

  • 2012: First one-day workshop held in Krakow, Poland.
  • 2013: ISNET: Information and Statistics in Nuclear Experiment and Theory, held in Glasgow, Scotland, UK.
    Organizers: Arnau Rios Huguet, David Ireland, David Jenkins, Paul Stevenson, Rolf-Dietmar Herzberg, Witek Nazarewicz
  • 2015: Information and statistics in nuclear experiment and theory ISNET-3, held in Trento, Italy
    Organizers: David Ireland, Witek Nazarewicz, Bartlomiej Szpak
  • 2016: Bayesian Methods in Nuclear Physics, held in Seattle, Washington, USA.
    Organizers: Dick Furnstahl, Dave Higdon, Nicolas Schunck, Andrew Steiner
  • 2017: Information and statistics in nuclear experiment and theory (ISNET-5) held in York, England.
    Organizers: Jacek Dobaczewski, David Ireland, Witek Nazarewicz
  • 2018: Uncertainty Quantification at the Extremes (ISNET-6) held in Darmstadt, Germany.
    Organizers: Dick Furnstahl, David Ireland, Daniel Phillips, Ian Vernon.
  • 2019: Bayesian Inference in Subatomic Physics: A Marcus Wallenberg Symposium held in Gothenburg, Sweden.
    Organizers: Andreas Ekström, Christian Forssén, Sarah Wesolowski.
  • 2020 The next ISNET meeting is expected to be held in North America, at Michigan State University in East Lansing, MI, USA.
